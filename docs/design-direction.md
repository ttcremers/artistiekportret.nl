# Design direction

## Desired feeling

The site should feel like a quiet, modern atelier in The Hague in late-afternoon light, with one portrait resting against the wall.

It should be warm, calm, handcrafted and unhurried. Not glossy. Not aggressively commercial. Not decorative for decoration's sake.

## Artistic references

Use these references as a direction of travel rather than as a costume box:

- **Vilhelm Hammershøi** for stillness, space and muted tones
- **Johannes Vermeer** for soft natural light and intimacy
- **Rembrandt** for warmth, depth and materiality
- **Irving Penn** for simplicity and authority
- **Paolo Roversi** for photographic painterliness and softness
- **Teun Hocks** for a subtle undercurrent of estrangement and gentle absurdity

Editorial-gallery additions:

- **IGNANT** for editorial rhythm, varied image scales, deliberate pauses, asymmetry and image-led composition
- **Space Copenhagen** for warm richness, background colour, typography and tactile calm
- **White Cube** for art-gallery authority and confidence in presenting work as work
- **Shibui** for wabi-sabi restraint and the discipline to remove unnecessary interface elements
- **Gallery Hugo theme** for portfolio browsing behaviour and quiet image-opening transitions
- **Nona Inescu** and **Bridget** for one authored or slightly unexpected gesture, while keeping the visitor oriented
- **Kyle McDougall** for breathing room and deliberate visual silence, but not for an almost-empty commercial landing page

## Visual principles

- warm off-white backgrounds rather than bright digital white
- near-black or dark grey text
- restrained muted accents such as olive, slate or rust
- a bright, warm yellow-gold accent used sparingly, like a patch of late afternoon sunlight rather than luxury-brand gold
- elegant serif headings
- practical sans-serif body text
- generous whitespace
- large but unhurried images
- occasional photographs of framed physical works in real interiors
- subtle tactile cues where useful: paper, wood, print, fabric and frame

## Avoid

- autoplay carousels
- busy motion and attention-seeking animation
- ornate faux-historical decoration
- excessive gold
- generic photography-site tropes
- glossy premium-package language
- visual clutter

## UI direction

Navigation should be clear and calm. The design should support a visitor who may already feel uncertain about being photographed. The site must not rush, corner or overwhelm them.

Prefer a small number of meaningful actions:

- view portraits
- understand the working method
- read about the maker
- make contact

Each page should have enough space for the images and text to breathe.
